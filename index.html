📋 Performance Summary

Meta · May 4 – June 28 TikTok · May 11 – June 28 Walmart · Old Model (May) + New Model (June) Display · May 20 – June 28 Off Story · May 27 – June 28

The Dippin' Dots Spring/Summer 2026 paid media campaign is pacing well through eight weeks in market. Data reflects May 1 – June 28, 2026. Walmart's May results are preserved as final historical data due to a platform attribution model update on June 1; new Walmart campaigns are tracked separately.

Meta continues to deliver standout efficiency. The Engagement campaign has generated 25,224 post engagements at a $0.13 CPE with a 16.60% engagement rate — well above benchmarks for the category. The Traffic/Retargeting campaign has driven 9,076 landing page views at just $0.18 per visit with a 3.79% CTR — more than 6× the 0.56% Meta average. Frequency is at 1.75, well within healthy range.

TikTok has now surpassed 2.36M impressions at a sustained $1.82 CPM — less than half the $3–4+ benchmark — confirming strong creative-algorithm fit through eight weeks in market.

Walmart Old Model campaigns (May 12–31) closed at 2.40× blended ROAS on $20,017 in attributed sales — final results under the previous attribution model. New Model campaigns (June 1–21) have generated $10,368 in attributed sales on $8,729 in spend for a 1.19× blended ROAS — Contextual leading at 1.32× ROAS.

Display Custom/Carousel has now generated 8.60M impressions across 18 retail partners (May 20–June 28), with Kroger, Walmart, and Stop & Shop leading on volume. A new Off Story campaign launched May 27 across 11 retail partners, adding 582,428 impressions at a strong 1.36% CTR and 76.2% story completion rate.

Meta Ads — Creative Analysis

Engagement + Traffic · May 1 – June 28
✅ What's Working — Keep Running
  • Static V2 — Amusement Park concept is carrying the Engagement campaign: 25,224 total engagements at $0.13 CPE across a 16.6% engagement rate. The creative that should anchor the next brief.
  • Product Carousel is the clear winner in Traffic/Retargeting: 6,500 combined LPVs at $0.17 avg cost per LPV, with Above Average quality and engagement rankings from Meta. The format resonates — use it as the foundation for the next retargeting brief.

⏸️ What's Not Landing
  • OOH Carousel — $0.20 avg cost per LPV across both ad sets vs $0.17 for Product Carousel. Lower volume and higher cost with no quality advantage. The OOH format isn't generating the same traction — the creative concept isn't driving action at the retargeting stage.
  • Carousel V1 — only 598 combined LPVs total. Negligible delivery and the algorithm hasn't picked it up in 8 weeks. After 8 weeks the algorithm hasn't picked it up — the format and concept aren't connecting.

TikTok — Creative Analysis

Reach Campaign · May 11 – June 28
✅ What's Working — Keep Running
  • Native Concept 4 is the dominant reach driver with 338K reach at $1.75 CPM — well below the $3–4 benchmark and by far the highest-volume creative in the mix.
  • Native Concepts 1, 2, 3, and 5 are the most CPM-efficient formats ($1.33–$1.43). The algorithm clearly favors native-style content. These should be the template for any new creative development.

Market Performance

Retail Syndicated Data · Jan – Jun 2026
Awareness and reach channels like Meta, TikTok, and Display are designed to build brand recognition and influence purchase decisions at shelf — not to generate a trackable click-to-buy. We believe this media is doing its job. To show why, we track velocity (average weekly dollar sales per store), which is the most honest measure of whether real demand is building. Unlike total sales, velocity isn't inflated by new stores — it tells you how hard each location is working. The trend here is encouraging.
⚠️ Distribution context: Wide retail distribution launched in May 2026. The jump in total dollar sales from Q1 to May is largely explained by a major increase in stores carrying the product — from ~17% ACV in January to ~28% in June. The velocity trend below controls for this and is the more meaningful demand signal.
